What is Liver Fibrosis?

Scar Tissue Formation in the Liver

Liver fibrosis occurs when repeated liver injury (fatty liver, hepatitis, alcohol, toxins) causes scar tissue. Early detection helps prevent progression to cirrhosis and liver failure.

About Liver Fibrosis

Liver fibrosis is the gradual replacement of healthy liver tissue with scar tissue due to chronic injury. Over time, fibrosis can disrupt liver structure and blood flow, leading to cirrhosis (F4) and complications. Early-stage fibrosis (F1–F2) can often be managed or slowed with timely medical care and lifestyle changes.
