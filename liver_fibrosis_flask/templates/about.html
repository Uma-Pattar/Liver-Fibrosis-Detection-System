About Liver Fibrosis Staging

Liver fibrosis is a condition where scar tissue forms in the liver due to chronic injury (such as fatty liver disease, hepatitis infection, alcohol-related damage, or long-term inflammation). As fibrosis increases, the liver becomes less efficient, and advanced scarring can lead to cirrhosis (F4).

This project is an AI-based Liver Fibrosis Staging System that analyzes liver ultrasound images and predicts the fibrosis stage from F0 to F4 using a deep learning model (CNN). The system provides confidence scores, saves results in history, and generates a detailed report for each prediction.

Fibrosis Stages Summary
  • F0 – No fibrosis (normal liver tissue)
  • F1 – Mild fibrosis (early scarring)
  • F2 – Moderate fibrosis (increased scarring)
  • F3 – Severe fibrosis (bridging fibrosis)
  • F4 – Cirrhosis (advanced scarring and structural changes)
